## Task What is IoT

The following notes I also shared with Matias Jürgenson, and since it's the first lab with our introductions, we kept it seperate. We'll add links to each other's repos next time.

Internet of Things (IoT) refers to a network of physical objects (“things”) that are embedded with sensors, software, and connectivity so they can collect data, communicate, and act over the internet without constant human intervention. These devices can monitor environments, automate tasks, and support decision-making by sending data to other systems or the cloud.

Domains and included areas:

- Infrastructure: traffic monitoring, smart lighting, environmental sensors, smart parking.
- Consumer IoT: devices used in everyday life like wearables, smart appliances, home security, (smart homes).
- Industrial IoT: Used in industry and manufacturing to improve efficiency and safety. Examples: factory sensors, predictive maintenance systems, smart grids.
- AI: magic wand demo spells


Commonly used (data) protocols:

- MQTT (Message Queuing Telemetry Transport): Lightweight publish/subscribe messaging protocol designed for low bandwidth and unreliable networks.
- CoAP (Constrained Application Protocol): REST-like protocol optimized for constrained devices and low-power networks.
- HTTP is also common: clients make requests GET, PUT, DELETE, POST.

Typical devices (appliance or micro controller):

- Smart Appliance. Example: Smart thermostat or smart light bulb, which collects sensor data and can be remotely controlled.
- Microcontroller Board. Example: ESP32 or Arduino or Raspberry Pi - small programmable controllers used to build IoT sensors and devices with Wi-Fi/Bluetooth connectivity.
